Each slice is split into five sections, corresponding to our priority areas. The index score of each priority area is illustrated by the intensity of the color. The regional organization and color variations aim to emphasize spatial patterns among the data. One D Scorecard Priority Area Heat Chart

This chart displays the individual indicators that comprise each priority area and drive the One D Scorecard. The value of an indicator is illustrated by the intensity of the color, coded to its priority area. The layout of the chart aims to illustrate how we calculate the comprehensive One D Index; each priority area makes up an equal portion of the total circle, and the individual indicators within a priority area are equally weighted slices, with the exception of indicators reported by race when a total slice is split into sectors. Learn more about our priority areas, the individual indicators that drive them, and how we calculate the One D Index in our Methodology.

Roll over any slice in the circle to begin exploring the data or choose an individual indicator to highlight. Change the region to compare data. Watch annual change using the time slider. Click Download for more information about the displayed data and source information.

Welcome to the One Detroit Portal

The state of Michigan’s future is tied to the future of Metro Detroit. This visualization allows a deep dive into the indicators essential for improving our region. Just as there are differences between CBSAs across the country, A look at the Detroit area census tract level data will reveal differences within our own region.

We are also happy to share with you another index. The Kirwan Institute has shared their opportunity index with us, so that we can better understand equity and access in Southeast Michigan.
